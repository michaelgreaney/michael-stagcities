Lisbon, Portugal

Why Lisbon?

Sunny, vibrant, and surprisingly affordable. Lisbon offers a coastal vibe with bustling city life. Great food, cheap Super Bock beer, friendly locals, and a legendary nightlife scene make it a top-tier stag destination. The weather, even in February, is likely to be milder than almost anywhere else on the list.

Stag Activities

  • Beer Bike Tour: Pedal through the city streets while sinking pints.
  • Go Karting: Unleash your competitive side on a high-speed track.
  • Surf Lessons: Hit the nearby coast and catch some waves.
  • Food & Wine Tasting: Experience Portuguese culture through its incredible food.

Nightlife

Start in the Bairro Alto district, a labyrinth of cobbled streets packed with over 100 tiny, eclectic bars. When those close, head down to Pink Street for the bigger clubs that stay open until the early hours. It's a guaranteed wild night out.

  • Sat, 7th Feb 2026: Sporting CP vs. FC Porto (Primeira Liga) - A massive derby!

Cologne, Germany

Why Cologne?

Famous for its incredible cathedral, liberal attitude, and unique Kölsch beer culture. Cologne offers a classic German stag experience. It's a city built for a good time, with traditional beer halls and a buzzing nightlife that caters to all tastes.

Stag Activities

  • Brewery Tour: Dive into the world of Kölsch beer, served in small glasses that are replaced until you say stop!
  • Rhine River Booze Cruise: See the city from the water with a beer in hand.
  • Bubble Football: Strap yourselves into giant inflatable balls and cause chaos.
  • Paintballing: A stag do classic in a top-notch German facility.

Nightlife

The action is centred around Zülpicher Strasse (the student quarter) for cheap drinks and a lively atmosphere, and the Belgian Quarter for trendier bars. The Old Town (Altstadt) is packed with traditional beer halls perfect for starting the evening.

⚽ Professional Football

  • Sat, 31st Jan 2026: 1. FC Köln vs. VfL Wolfsburg (Bundesliga)
  • Sat, 7th Feb 2026: 1. FC Köln vs. RB Leipzig (Bundesliga)

Warsaw, Poland

Why Warsaw?

A city of incredible history and resilience that has transformed into a modern, bustling metropolis. Warsaw offers fantastic value for money, a huge range of activities, and a seriously underrated and explosive nightlife, all powered by cheap, quality vodka.

Stag Activities

  • AK-47 Shooting Range: Fire some serious hardware, including Kalashnikovs.
  • Vodka Tasting: It's Poland. This is an essential cultural experience!
  • Party Bus/Tram: Your own private club on wheels to tour the city.
  • Escape Rooms: Test your wits before you lose them on the vodka.

Nightlife

The city centre is packed with 'pawilony' (pavilions), small courtyard bars perfect for pre-drinking. Afterwards, the area around Mazowiecka Street is where you'll find the biggest and best nightclubs, with many venues staying open past sunrise.

⚽ Professional Football

  • Sat, 31st Jan 2026: Legia Warsaw vs. Korona Kielce (Ekstraklasa)
  • Sat, 7th Feb 2026: Polonia Warsaw vs. Stal Mielec (I Liga)

Krakow, Poland

Why Krakow?

The undisputed king of stag do destinations for a reason. Krakow is stunningly beautiful, incredibly cheap, and absolutely jam-packed with activities. The density of bars and clubs in its Main Market Square and the Jewish Quarter of Kazimierz is legendary.

Stag Activities

  • Shooting Range: An essential Krakow stag activity with a wide range of weapons.
  • Vodka Tasting Tour: Learn about (and drink) Poland's national spirit.
  • White Water Rafting: An adrenaline-pumping day trip from the city.
  • Jelly Wrestling: Because, of course.

Nightlife

It's relentless. The Main Square is home to countless underground cellar bars and huge clubs. For a slightly more alternative, bohemian vibe, head to the Kazimierz district. Everything is within walking distance, making it perfect for a bar crawl.

Bucharest, Romania

Why Bucharest?

Nicknamed "Little Paris of the East," Bucharest is an energetic, chaotic, and fascinating city. It offers some of the best value in Europe, meaning your budget will stretch much further. The Old Town is a non-stop party zone, perfect for a stag weekend.

Stag Activities

  • Therme Bucuresti: A giant, modern thermal spa complex to sweat out the hangover.
  • Visit Dracula's Castle: A day trip to the legendary Bran Castle in Transylvania.
  • Shooting Range: Get your hands on some high-calibre firearms.
  • Steak & Strip: A classic stag do dinner with a Romanian twist.

Nightlife

The Old Town (Centru Vechi) is the heart of the action. It's a pedestrianised area crammed with hundreds of bars, pubs, and clubs. You can wander from one to the next all night long without needing a taxi. The vibe is electric and the party goes on late.

  • Fri, 21st Feb 2026: FC Rapid Bucuresti vs. Dinamo Bucureşti.

Sofia, Bulgaria

Why Sofia?

If you're looking for an off-the-beaten-track destination that offers unbelievable value, Sofia is your answer. It's a cool, modern city with a rich history, vibrant student population, and what is likely the cheapest beer on this entire list. A hidden gem for a stag do.

Stag Activities

  • Skiing/Snowboarding Day Trip: Vitosha Mountain is right on the city's doorstep.
  • Indoor Karting: A huge, modern indoor track for some racing action.
  • Clay Pigeon Shooting: Test your aim in the Bulgarian countryside.
  • Bar Crawl with a Local Guide: Discover the best hidden spots in the city.

Nightlife

Vitosha Boulevard is the main artery, lined with bars and restaurants. For a more intense party scene, head to the Studentski Grad (Students' City) district, where you'll find countless clubs (known as 'diskoteki') pumping out music until dawn at incredibly low prices.

⚽ Professional Football

  • Sat, 7th Feb 2026: Levski Sofia vs. Botev Vratsa (Parva Liga)
